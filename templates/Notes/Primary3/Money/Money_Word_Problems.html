How to Solve 2-Step Word Problems on Adding/Subtracting Money

Practice Unlimited Questions

1.  Sarah went to a movie theatre with $25.
She bought a movie ticket for $8.40 and a bottle of soda for $4.50.
How much money had she left?

$8.40  +  $4.50  =  $12.90
She spent $12.90 altogether.

$25  −  $12.90  =  $12.10
She had $12.10 left.

2. A bicycle costs $84.85.
A skateboard costs $36.40 less than the bicycle.
Jason buys two skateboards.
How much does he need to pay the cashier?

$84.85  −  $36.40  =  $48.45
The cost of a skateboard is $48.45.  (a)

$48.45  +  $48.45  =  $96.90
He needs to pay $96.90 to the cashier.

3. Maya has $9.25 at first.
Then she saves another $6.45. She wants to buy a school bag that costs $17.95.
How much more must she save to buy the school bag?

$9.25  +  $6.45  =  $15.70
She has $15.70 altogether.

$17.95  −  $15.70  =  $2.25
She must save another $2.25 to buy the school bag.

5. Timothy went to a stationery shop and bought a marker and a stapler.

The marker cost $3.45.
How much did the stapler cost?

$10  −  $2.35  =  $7.65
The marker and the stapler cost $7.65 altogether.

$7.65  −  $3.45  =  $4.20
The stapler cost $4.20.

How much money did Bryan have left?

Anna and Bryan each had $47.50 at first.

$47.50  −  $21.65  =  $25.85

7. A supermarket has coffee beans on sale.
A bag of coffee beans that has a usual price of $17.80 is selling at a discounted price of $12.40.
Rayan buys two such bags of coffee beans.
How much discount does he receive altogether?

$17.80  −  $12.40  =  $5.40
He receives a discount of $5.40 on each bag of coffee beans.  (a)

$5.40  +  $5.40 = $10.80
He receives a discount of $10.80 altogether.
