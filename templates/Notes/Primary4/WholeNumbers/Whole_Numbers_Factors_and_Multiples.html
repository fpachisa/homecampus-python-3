Factors and Multiples

Practice Unlimited Questions

What are factors?

Set of whole numbers when multiplied gives a product. Those whole numbers are called factors of the product.

Example 1: 3 × 5 = 15. 3 and 5 are factors of 15.

Example 2: 2 × 3 × 5 = 30. 2, 3 and 5 are factors of 30.
6 is also a factor of 30 since 6 × 5 = 30

What are multiples?

Product of one number multiplied by another number

Example 1: 2 × 1 = 2, 2 × 2 = 4, 2 × 3 = 6, 2 × 4 = 8, 2 × 5 = 10

2, 4, 6, 8, 10 are multipes of 2.

What is Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) or Greatest Common Factor (GCF)?

The greatest common divisor (GCD or GCF) of two numbers is the greatest number that divides both the numbers.

For e.g.: The factors of 6 are: 1, 2 and 3.
The factors of 8 are: 1, 2 and 4

The greatest number that divides both 6 and 8 is 2. Hence 2 is the GCD of 6 and 8.

What is Least Common Multiple (LCM)?

The least common multiple (LCM) of two numbers is the smallest number that is divisible by both the numbers.

For e.g.: The multiples of 2 are: 2, 4, 5, 8, 10.
The multiple of 3 are: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15

The smallest number that is divisible by both 2 and 3 is 6. Hence, 6 is LCM of 2 and 3.

1. Find the factors of 8.

8 is the product of 1 and 8.
So 1 and 8 are factors of 8.

2 × 4 = 8
8 is the product of 2 and 4.
So, 2 and 4 are factors of 8.

The factors of 8 are 1, 2, 4 and 8.
1 is a factor of every number.
Every number is a factor of itself.
