4. Shelly had {{ mixNumGenQ(3,1,2) }} kg of peanuts.
She sold     {{ frGen(5,7) }} of them.
Find the mass of peanuts she had left.

1  −  {{ frGen(5,7) }}  =  {{ frGen(2,7) }}
She had {{ frGen(2,7) }}  of the peanuts left.

    {{ frGen(2,7) }}  of  {{ mixNumGen(3,1,2) }}  kg
=  {{ frGen(2,7) }}  ×  {{ mixNumGen(3,1,2) }}  kg
=  {{ frGen(2,7) }}  ×  {{ frGen(7,2) }}  kg
=  {{ frGen("2 x 7","7 x 2") }} kg
=   1 kg

The mass of peanuts she had left was 1 kg.

5. Barry has 36 coins.
{{ frGen(1,2) }} of them are Singapore coins,     {{ frGen(1,3) }} of them are US coins and the rest are Indian coins.
How many Indian coins does he have?

Change {{ frGen(1,2) }} and {{ frGen(1,3) }} to like fractions.
{{ frGen(1,2) }}  =  {{ frGen(3,6) }}
{{ frGen(1,3) }}  =  {{ frGen(2,6) }}
6 units   36
1 unit     36  ÷  6  =  6

He has 6 Indian coins.

6. Mei had a piece of ribbon.
She used     {{ frGen(3,4) }} of it to make bows.
She used 60 cm of the ribbon to make bows.
Find the length of the ribbon she had at first.

3 units   60 cm
1 unit     60 cm  ÷  3  =  20 cm
4 units   20 cm  ×  4  =  80 cm

She had 80 cm of ribbon at first.

7. Sam received $150 of pocket money.
He spent     {{ frGen(2,5) }} of it on a pair of shoes and     {{ frGen(1,6) }} of the remaining on a watch.
a.   How much did the shoes cost?
b.   Find the sum of money he had left in the end?
c.   What fraction of his pocket money had he left?

a.   5 units   $150
      1 unit     $150  ÷  5  =  $30
      2 units   $30  ×  2  =  $60

      The shoes cost  $60.
b.   $150  −  $60  =  $90
      6 units   $90
      1 unit     $90  ÷  6  =  $15
      5 units   $15  ×  5  =  $75

      He had  $75  left in the end.
c.   Sum of money left in the end  =  $75
      Sum of pocket money at first  =  $150
      Fraction of pocket money left  =  {{ frGen(75,150) }}  =  {{ frGen(1,2) }}
      He had {{ frGen(1,2) }} of his pocket money left.
