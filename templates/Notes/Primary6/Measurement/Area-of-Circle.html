What is Area of Circle?

Area of a cirlce   =   π   ×   r2

The space occupied by a circle in 2-dimensional space is the area of a circle. For derivation of the formula see the section below.

Use this area calculator for more formulas and ready answers.

Derivation of the formula for area of a circle.

Trace a circular shape on paper and cut it along the edge. Fold it in half, then fold it 3 more times so you have 16 folding lines meeting at the centre like below.

Cut the circle along the folding lines to get 16 slices (sectors) and arrange them as below.

The circle is roughly arranged into a rectangle so their areas are now the same.

Area of the circle = Area of the rectangle
= Length × Breadth
= π × Radius × Radius
Area of Circle = π × Radius × Radius

2. The diameter of a circular frisbee is 24 cm. Find its area. Give your answer in terms of π.


Diameter  =  24 cm
Radius  =  24 ÷ 2
=  12 cm
Area  =  π × 12 × 12
=  144 π cm2

3. The diameter of a semicircle is 5 cm. What is its area?


Radius  =  5 cm ÷ 2
=  2.5 cm

Area of a semicircle  =  Half of the area of the circle
=  {{ frGen(1,2) }}× 3.14 × 2.5 × 2.5
=  9.81 cm2
