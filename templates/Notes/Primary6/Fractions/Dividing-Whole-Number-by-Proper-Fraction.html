How to divide a whole number by a proper fraction?

Dividing a whole number by a proper fraction is the same as multiplying the whole number by the inverse of the proper fraction. Here are the steps:

Step 1:   Change  ÷  to  × .
Step 2:   Invert the fraction.
Step 3:   Simplify the expression.

Example:
Divide 8 by {{ frGen(4,5) }}.
8  ÷  {{ frGen(4,5) }}  =  8  ×  {{ frGen(5,4) }}  =  10

6. Mr. Lee buys 25 kg of decorative shells from a wholesale market.

a)   How many bags does he get?
b)   If he sells each bag at $3.50, how much money does he collect from the sale of all bags?


a)     25  ÷  {{ frGen(1,4) }}
    =  25  ×  {{ frGen(4,1) }}
    =  100

    He gets 100 bags.

b)     $3.50  ×  100  =  $350

    He collects $350.

      There are {{ frGen(54,11) }} litres of perfume in the 27 bottles altogether.

      5  −  {{ frGen(54,11) }}
      =  {{ frGen("55 - 54",11) }}
      =  {{ frGen(1,11) }} litre
      The bottle contains {{ frGen(1,11) }} litre of perfume.
