2. What are Equivalent Fractions?

Fractions that are equal to each other are called equivalent fractions.

3. Why should we simplify fractions?

We should simplify fractions because simplified fractions are easier to understand.

Example:
So, though {{ frGen(51,85) }} is the same as {{ frGen(3,5) }}, it is much easier to understand {{ frGen(3,5) }} than {{ frGen(51,85) }}.

4. Simplify the fraction   {{ frGen(4,8) }}.

To simplify the fraction, keep dividing the numerator and the denominator by a common factor until you can't divide them any further.

So, to simplify the fraction {{ frGen(4,8) }}, divide both 4 and 8 by 2 (as 2 is a common factor of 4 and 8).
{{ frGen("4 / 2","8 / 2") }}  =  {{ frGen(2,4) }}

2 and 4 can be divided further by 2.

{{ frGen("2 / 2","4 / 2") }}  =  {{ frGen(1,2) }}

{{ frGen(1,2) }} is the simplest form of the fraction {{ frGen(4,8) }}.

5. Simplify the fraction   {{ frGen(180,240) }}.

To simplify the fraction {{ frGen(180,240) }}, divide both 180 and 240 by their common factors.

It is easy to see that both numbers can be divided by 10.

{{ frGen("180 / 10","240 / 10") }}  =  {{ frGen(18,24) }}

18 and 24 can be divided further by 2.

{{ frGen("18 / 2","24 / 2") }}  =  {{ frGen(9,12) }}

9 and 12 can be divided further by 3.

{{ frGen("9 / 3","12 / 3") }}  =  {{ frGen(3,4) }}

{{ frGen(3,4) }} is the simplest form of the fraction {{ frGen(180,240) }}.
