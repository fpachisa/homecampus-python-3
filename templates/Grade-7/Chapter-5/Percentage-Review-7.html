5.1.3 Comparing Quantities by Percentage
Example 9:
During a carnival, a confectioner sold 2500 pieces of red, blue, yellow and pink candies. 25% of the total candies sold were red while 30% of them were blue. The number of yellow candies sold was 10% more than the number of blue candies. The rest of the candies sold were pink in color.
a) What percentage of the total candies sold were pink?
b) The most number of candies sold were of which colour? What percentage of the total candies sold were these?
