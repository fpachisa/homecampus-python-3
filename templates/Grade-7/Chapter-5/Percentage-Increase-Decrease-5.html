5.3.2 Concept of Percentage Point
Example 19:
A man borrows $50000 from a bank at an interest rate of 6% of the loan amount per month. Due to some change in the loan scheme, the bank now charges interest at the rate of 8.5% per month. Find the percentage increase in the interest.

Solution 19:
The original interest paid
  =  
6% of $50000

  =  
$3000

The new interest paid
  =  
8.5% of $50000

  =  
$4250

Increase in monthly interest
= 
4250 − 3000

 
$1250

Percentage increase
= 

1250
___

3000

× 100 %

= 
41.67 %
Do note..
Percentage point is the difference between two percentages which is 2.5% (= 8.5% − 6%) in the above example. It is different from the actual percentage increase which is 41.67% in this case.
