Example 7:
A joint family has 15 male and 22 female members. Find:
a) Percentage of male members in the family
b) Percentage of female members in the family

Solution 7:
Total number of members in the family:
= 15 + 22 = 37
% male =

15
____

37

× 100 = 40.54%

% female =

22
____

37

× 100 = 59.46%
The "whole" family is made up of two "parts" - male members and female members. The sum of the percentages of the two parts i.e. 40.54 + 59.46 must equal 100%.
In such cases, if we know the percentage value of one part, say 40.54%, we can find the percentage value of the other part by subtracting the known value from 100% as follows:
100 − 40.54 = 59.46%
