An algebraic expression consists of numbers and variables along with operation symbols (+, −, × and ÷). It may also contain brackets.
Example 2:
A man distributed an equal sum of money among 3 children. How much money did each child get if the total amount distributed was:

a)  $270
b)  $3300
c)  $z

Solution 2:
a) Money received by each child
= $270 ÷ 3

= $90

b) Money received by each child
= $3300 ÷ 3

= $1100

c) Money received by each child
= $z ÷ 3
Here, z ÷ 3 is an algebraic expression in which z is a variable.
