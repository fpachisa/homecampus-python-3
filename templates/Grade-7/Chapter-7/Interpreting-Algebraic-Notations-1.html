7.2 Interpreting Algebraic Notations
An algebraic expression is made up of algebraic terms.
For example, x + 2y − 3z + 4 consists of 4 algebraic terms which are x, 2y, −3z and 4.
The numeric part with each variable is called its coefficient. In the above example, 1, 2 and −3 are the coefficients of x, y and z.
The term 4 is called a constant because it has no variable attached to it. This means that it does not change with the change in value of any variable.
Do Note:
Coefficients and constants include the sign that is in front of them.
