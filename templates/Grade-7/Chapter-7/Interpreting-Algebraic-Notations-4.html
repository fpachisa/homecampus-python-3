b)
Price of p burgers
= $3 × p

Price of 4 hot dogs
= $1 × 4

∴ Total price
= $(3 × p + 1 × 4)

= $(3p + 4)
When p is multiplied by 3, it is written as 3p rather than 3×p or p3.
c)
Price of m burgers
= $3 × m

Price of n hot dogs
= $1 × n

∴ Total price
= $(3 × m + 1 × n)

= $(3m + n)
When the coefficient is 1, we skip the coefficient and write only the variable. 1n is written simply as n.
In the above example, m, n and p are variables.
3p + 4 or 4 + 3p is an algebraic expression in terms of p. In the algebraic term 3p, 3 is called the coefficient of p and 4 is a constant.
3m + n or n + 3m is an algebraic expression in terms of 2 variables m and n. In the algebraic term 3m, 3 is called the coefficient of m while in the algebraic term n, the coefficient of n is 1.
