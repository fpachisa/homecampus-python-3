2.4.5 Real Numbers
A real number is a value that can be expressed on a number line.
The following numbers can be represented by points on the number line:
  • Rational Numbers - Include integers, fractions, terminating decimals and repeating decimals.
  • Irrational numbers - Include non-terminating and non-recurring decimals. E.g.: √2  (=1.41421356...*), √3  (=1.73205080...*), π (=3.14159265358979...*).
What is NOT a Real Number?
  • Imaginary numbers like √−2  (square root of minus two) i.e. √−x , where x is a positive number.
  • Infinity (∞) - It has no end. Example: (10)∞ means 10 to the power of infinity i.e. 1 followed by an endless string of zeros. Hence, it is not defined.
* Only a finite number of digits are shown here.
