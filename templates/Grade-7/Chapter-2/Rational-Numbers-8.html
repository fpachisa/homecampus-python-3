2.4.4 Decimals
There are two types of decimals that are rational numbers:
  1. Terminating decimals: These are decimals that have a finite number of digits. They are rational numbers because they can be expressed as fractions.
    Eg.: 3

    3
    __

    4

    =
    3.75,

    7
    __

    5

    =
    1.4
  2. Recurring or Repeating decimals: These are decimals that have an infinite number of repeating blocks. E.g.:

    1. 1
      __

      3

      =
      0.333 or 0.3

      (repeating blocks of 3 indicated by a bar above '3')

    2. 9
      __

      11

      =
      0.8181 or 0.81

      (repeating blocks of 81 indicated by a bar above '81')
There is a third category of decimals called non-terminating and non-recurring decimals that are not rational numbers but are instead categorized as irrational numbers. They are discussed in the next section.
