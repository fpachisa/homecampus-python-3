Example 18:
a) Is (4)2 different from (−4)2?
b) Is (5)3 different from (−5)3?
c) Find √4 , ∛27 , ∛(−27) 
Solution 18:
a)
(4)2 = 4 × 4 = 16
(−4)2 = (−4) × (−4) = 16
So, (4)2 = (−4)2
We can also say that,
√16  = ±4 (± stands for '+' or '−')
b)
(5)3 = 125
(−5)3 = −125
So, (5)3 ≠ (−5)3
∛125  = 5 and ∛−125  = −5
c)
√4  = √2×2  or √(−2×−2)  = ±2
∛27  = ∛(3×3×3)  = 3
∛−27  = ∛(−3×−3×−3)  = −3
