2.3.3 Combined Four Operations of Integers
We learnt about the rules of the order of operations on whole numbers in lower classes. The same rules apply to operations on integers. The acronym BEMDAS explained below denotes the order of operations.
B - Brackets: First, evaluate the expressions within the brackets, starting from the innermost brackets.
E - Exponents: Next, evaluate the exponents, indexes or powers.
MD - Multiplication and Division: Then, multiply and divide from left to right.
AS - Addition and Subtraction: Lastly, add or subtract from left to right.
Example 22:
Evaluate the following:
a) (−10) × 5 ÷ 2
b) 51 ÷ (−3) + 12 × (−3)
c) 1 − (−75) ÷ 5 − (−4)
d) 11 × (−3)2 − 22
