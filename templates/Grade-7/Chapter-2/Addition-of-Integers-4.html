2.2.2 Adding two integers with different signs
Example 11:
An ice cream initially at a temperature of −3°C melts as the temperature increases by 7°C. What is the temperature of the melted ice cream?
Solution 11:
Temperature of melted ice cream = −3 + 7
Start at negative 3 on the number line and move +7 units to the right. The answer is +4.
Alternative method:
When adding two integers with the different sign, First, subtract the smaller absolute value (3) from the larger (7).
In the example above, 7 − 3 = 4.
