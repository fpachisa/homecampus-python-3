2.1.1 Negative Numbers
You would have noticed the daily weather records in newspapers giving temperature readings of −5 °C, −24 °C, +17 °C, +39 °C, etc.
Note the '+' and '−' signs before the numbers. −24 °C means the temperature is 24 °C below 0 °C while +17 °C means the temperature is 17 °C above 0 °C.
Good to know...
On a thermometer, 0 °C is the point at which water freezes. 0 °C is considered as the standard reference point for measuring temperatures.
A number that is less than zero is called a negative number. It is written with a 'minus' sign in front. For example, −5 is a negative number with a magnitude of 5. It is read as 'minus five' or 'negative five'.
