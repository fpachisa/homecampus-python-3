9.1 Linear Equations in One Variable
9.1.1   Definition of Linear Equation
6 + x = 9 is a linear equation in one variable. The '=' sign in the equation indicates that the total values on both sides must be equal.
A linear equation in one variable takes the form
ax + b = c, where a, b and c are constants and a ≠ 0.
Do Note:
6 + x is a linear equation in the variable 'x'.

The two sides of an equation are left hand side or LHS and right hand side or RHS.
Here,
LHS = 6 + x and RHS = 9.
x takes up a value that makes the equation true. It is known as the solution or root of the equation.
