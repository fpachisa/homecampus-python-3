In other words...
Cubes and cube roots can be thought of, respectively, as the volume and length of a cube.
For a cube with length n,
Volume = Length × Length × Length
= n × n × n = n3
Given the volume of a cube, we can find its length as below.
Length of side = ∛Area = ∛n3 = n
Numbers whose cube roots are whole numbers are called perfect cubes. The numbers 1, 8, 27, 64, 125, ... are perfect cubes. The cube roots of perfect cubes can be determined using prime factorisation.
Example 15:
Find the value of ∛216 .

Solution 15:
Write the prime factors of 216.
216  =  23 × 33
Divide the prime factors into 3 equal groups.
216 = (2 × 3) × (2 × 3) × (2 × 3) = (2 × 3)3
∴ ∛216   =  2 × 3  =  6
