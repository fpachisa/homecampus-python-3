We know that 3 cannot evenly divide 323. Hence, we can say that 9, which can be factored into 3 × 3, also cannot evenly divide 323.
Notice that 9 is a composite number with factors that are prime numbers (3 and 3).
The same is true about all composite numbers - they can be broken down into factors that are primes.
So, if a whole number is not divisible by a given prime number, then it will also not be divisible by any composite number of which the prime number is a factor. Therefore, to find out whether a given whole number is a prime, we only need to check if it is evenly divisible by prime numbers smaller than it.

323 is not evenly divisible by 11.
