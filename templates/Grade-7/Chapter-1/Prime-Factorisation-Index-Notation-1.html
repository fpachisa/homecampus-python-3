1.3.1 Prime Factorisation and Factor Trees
We learnt in the previous section that a composite number is a product of prime numbers. Or, in other words, a composite number can be broken down into factors of prime numbers. Such a factor is called a prime factor of the composite number.
70 = 2 × 5 × 7
Here, 70 is a composite number which can be broken into the prime factors, 2, 5 and 7.
Expressing a composite number as a product of its prime factors is called prime factorisation. We can use a special diagram called a factor tree to break down a number by dividing it by its factors until all the factors left are primes.
