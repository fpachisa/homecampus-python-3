Example 5:
Is 323 a prime or a composite number?

Solution 5:
We know that a number is not a prime if we are able to divide it by numbers other than 1 and itself. So, let's divide 323 by numbers smaller than it, one by one.
323 is an odd number, so it cannot be divided by even numbers. We should focus on dividing it by odd numbers.
323 ÷ 3 = 107 R 2
323 is not evenly divisible by 3. Next odd number is 5.
323 cannot be divided by 5 as it does not end in either 5 or 10. Next odd number is 7.
323 ÷ 7 = 46 R 1
323 is not evenly divisible by 7. Next odd number is 9.
