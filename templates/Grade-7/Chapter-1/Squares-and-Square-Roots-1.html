1.6 Square and Square Roots
We learnt in Section 1.3 that in index notation, 5 × 5 is 52 or 5 squared or 25. So, 25 is called the square of 5.
The square root of a number is a value, which when multiplied by itself, gives you the original number. The positive square root of 25 is 5. It is written as √25  = 5.
The square root symbol ( √) is called the radical.
So,
