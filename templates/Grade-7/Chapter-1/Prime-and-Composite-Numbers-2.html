Do you notice something special about the numbers in Table 1? Each of the numbers in Table 1 has only two factors, 1 and itself. Such numbers are called prime numbers.
In other words...
A whole number that cannot be divided evenly by any other whole number except 1 and itself is a prime number.
All other whole numbers that have more than two factors, i.e. the numbers in Table 2, are called composite numbers.
In other words...
A whole number that can be divided evenly by whole numbers other than simply 1 or itself is a composite number.
Fact...
The numbers 0 and 1 are considered neither prime nor composite.
