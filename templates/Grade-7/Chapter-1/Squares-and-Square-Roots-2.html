In other words...
Squares and square roots can be thought of, respectively, as the area and the length of a square.
For a square with length n,
Area = Length × Length = n × n = n2
Given the area of a square, we can find its length as below.
Length of side = √Area = √n2 = n
Numbers whose square roots are whole numbers are called perfect squares. The numbers 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, ... are perfect squares. The square roots of perfect squares can be determined using prime factorisation.
Example 13:
Find the value of √324 .

Solution 13:
Write the prime factors of 324.
324  =  22 × 34
Divide the prime factors into 2 equal groups.
324  =  (2 × 32) × (2 × 32)  =  (2 × 32)2
∴ √324   =  2 × 32  =  18
