Step 1:
Break 660 into any two factors, say 2 and 330.
Step 2:
Work these factors down to smaller numbers, if possible.
Step 3:
When you reach the point where you cannot factorise the numbers anymore, the resulting factors will be all prime. The product of all these prime factors is the prime factorisation of 660.
