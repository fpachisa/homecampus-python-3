1.7 Cubes and Cube Roots
We learnt in Section 1.3 that in index notation, 5 × 5 × 5 is 53 or 5 cubed or 125. So, 125 is called the cube of 5.
The cube root of a number is a value, which when cubed (multiplied by itself 3 times), gives you the original number. The cube root of 125 is 5. It is written as ∛125  = 5.
