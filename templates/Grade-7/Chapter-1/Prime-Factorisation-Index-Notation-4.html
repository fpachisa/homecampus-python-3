1.3.2 Index Notation
The index (also known as exponent or power) of a number tells us how many times to multiply the number by itself.
5 × 5
=
52 (called 5 to the power of 2 or 5 squared)
5 × 5 × 5
=
53 (called 5 to the power of 3 or 5 cubed)
5 × 5 × 5 × 5
=
54 (called 5 to the power of 4)
Consider 5 to the power of 3 or 53. Here, the number 5 is known as the base and 3 is known as the index or exponent. So, in the index notation, the index tells us how many times to multiply the base by itself.
