8.1 Addition and Subtraction of Linear Expressions
8.1.1 Like and Unlike Terms
Algebraic terms in an algebraic expression are of two types:
Like terms and Unlike terms.
Like terms are those algebraic terms that consist of the same variables and their exponents. However, their coefficients can be different.
Do Note:
All constant terms are like terms.
Consider the following examples of like terms: 3y and 13y, 2x2 and −14x2, 2ab2c and −3acb2.
Like terms can be grouped together to simplify an algebraic expression.
Unlike terms, on the other hand, have different variables and cannot be grouped together in an algebraic expression.
Consider the following examples of unlike terms: 6ab4c and 2abc4, 2x and 3y, 12a and 15, −mn2 and m2n.
