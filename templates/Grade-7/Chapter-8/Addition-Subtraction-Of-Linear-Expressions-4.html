8.1.2 Linear Expressions
A linear expression is an algebraic expression in which each term has at most one variable raised to the power of 1.
The following are examples of linear expressions:
2x − 2, m + n, 3r + 2s − 5t
Algebraic expressions containing terms that have more than one variable are non-linear in nature, even if each variable is raised to the power of 1. For example, 2xy and 3pqr are non-linear expressions.
Good to know...
Algebraic expressions that have terms in which variables are raised to the power of 2 are termed as quadratic expressions. Example, 2m2+z2, a2+3x2
