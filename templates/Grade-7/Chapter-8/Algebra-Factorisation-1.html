8.3 Factorisation

We learnt in a previous lesson about prime factorization wherein composite numbers can be expressed as a product of their prime factors.

For instance, 210 can be factorised into the following prime factors:

210 = 2 × 3 × 4 × 5

Similarly, an algebraic expression can be expressed as a product of other algebraic expressions.

For example, 4ab − 6ac can be factorised into two factors, 2a and (2b − 3c) as follows:

4ab − 6ac = 2a(2b − 3c)
