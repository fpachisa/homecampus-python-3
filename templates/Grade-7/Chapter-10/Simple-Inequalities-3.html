10.1.1 Solving Inequalities

Just like linear equations, inequalities contain variables. The values of the variables that satisfy the inequality are called the solutions of the inequality.

While finding the solutions, the inequality sign may either remain the same or reverse as shown in the examples below.

Example 2:
8 > 6. In which of the following cases does the inequality sign get reversed?

a)
When both sides are multiplied by 3.
b)
When both sides are multiplied by −5.
c)
When both sides are divided by 2.
d)
When both sides are divided by −4.
