Solution 2:
a)
8 > 6
Multiply both sides by 3.
LHS: 8 × 3 = 24
RHS: 6 × 3 = 18
24 > 18, as 24 appears to the right of 18 on the number line.
So, the inequality sign remains the same.
b)
8 > 6
Multiply both sides by −5.
LHS: 8 × −5 = −40
RHS: 6 × −5 = −30
−40 < −30, as −40 appears to the left of −30 on the number line.
So, the inequality sign gets reversed.
c)
8 > 6
Divide both sides by 2.
LHS: 8 ÷ 2 = 4
RHS: 6 ÷ 2 = 2
4 > 2, as 4 appears to the right of 2 on the number line.
So, the inequality sign remains the same.
