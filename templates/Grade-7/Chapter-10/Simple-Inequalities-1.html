10.1 Introduction to Simple Inequalities
The annual income of Mary is $50 more than twice that of Amy. If $x is the annual income of Mary and $y is the annual income of Amy, a linear equation can be formed as below,
x = 2y + 50
Here, the exact relationship between the two variables is known.
On the other hand, if it is said that the annual income of Mary, $x, is more than that of Amy, $y, it is represented as,
x > y
This is called an inequality. Here, the exact relationship between the two variables is unknown.
