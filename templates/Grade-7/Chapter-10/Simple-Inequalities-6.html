Example 4:
Solve 7 > 2y + 1.
Solution 4:
It is a common practice to write the variable term on LHS. The inequality sign reverses when LHS and RHS terms are swapped.
2y + 1
< 7
2y + 1 − 1
< 7 − 1
(Subtract 1 from both sides)
2y
< 6
2y ÷ 2
< 6 ÷ 2
(Divide both sides by 2)
y
< 3
This is the solution.
Example 5:
Solve 5 − 4x > −7.
Solution 5:
5 − 4x
> −7
5 − 4x − 5
> −7 − 5
(Subtract 5 from both sides)
−4x
> −12
−4x ÷ (−4)
< −12 ÷ (−4)
(Divide both sides by −4)
x
< 3
This is the solution.
(The inequality sign reverses when both sides are divided by a negative number.)
