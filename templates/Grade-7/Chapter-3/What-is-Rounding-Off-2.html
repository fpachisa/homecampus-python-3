Rules of rounding off whole numbers...
  • Round up: If the digit to the right of rounding digit is 5, 6, 7, 8 or 9, add one to the rounding digit. All the digits to the right of the rounding digit are changed to 0.
  • Round down: If the digit to the right of rounding digit is 0, 1, 2, 3 or 4, keep the rounding digit unchanged. All the digits to the right of it are changed to 0.
Example 1:
The population of the world in the year 2014 was 7,211,239,210. What is the population correct to the nearest:
a) 100   b) 10000   c) million

Solution 1:
a) 7 211 239 210
Rounding digit is digit in the hundreds place which is 2.
Digit to the right of rounding digit = 1
Since 1 < 5, the rounding digit is unchanged.
∴The population is 7 211 239 200, correct to the nearest 100.
