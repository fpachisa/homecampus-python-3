3.3 What is Estimation?
Estimation is the process of finding an approximate value even if the available data is incomplete or uncertain.
Various techniques of estimating are discussed below.
Example 12:
I have $35 in my pocket. Is this money sufficient to buy a bottle of 2 litres oil worth $12.76, a packet of chocolate cookies worth $9.32 and a can of orange juice worth $10.90? Find an estimated answer without using a calculator.

Solution 12:
Estimation can be done by rounding off each price to the nearest dollar.
$12.76 ≈ $13, $9.32 ≈ $9, $10.90 ≈ $11
Total price of 3 items
= $12.76 + $9.32 + $10.90

≈ $13 + $9 + $11 = $33
Estimated value, $33 is less than $35. Therefore, $35 is enough money to buy the desired items.
