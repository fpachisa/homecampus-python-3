6.2.2 Unit Conversion
The common units of speed are km/h (kilometers per hour) and m/s (metres per second). In general, speeds of objects that move fast and cover long distances, such as cars, are measured in km/h. On the other hand, when the movement is slow and the distance covered is short, such as the speed at which a person walks, we measure the speed in m/s. A more uncommon unit of speed, cm/s, is used for very slow moving objects which cover very small distances. Can you think of any such object?
Interesting fact
Light travels at a speed of 299 792 458 m/s ~ 2.9 × 108 m/s.
To compare the speeds measured in different units, we need to convert them from one unit to the other. The following examples explain how to do that.
Example 7:
A car is moving at a speed of 90 km/h. What is its speed in m/s?
Solution 7
Speed of the car
= 90 km/h

= 90 × 1000 metre/h

= (90 × 1000) ÷ 3600 m/sec
