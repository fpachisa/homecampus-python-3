Example 2:
A fruit basket contains 5 pears, 2 plums and 3 peaches.

Ratio of the number of pears to the number of plums = 5:2

Ratio of the number of plums to the number of peaches = 2:3

Ratio of the number of pears to the number of peaches = 5:3

All the ratios above compare one part to another. They are called Part to Part ratios.

Consider the ratios below:

Ratio of the number of pears to the total number of fruits

= 5:10 or 1:2

Ratio of the number of plums to the total number of fruits

= 2:10 or 1:5

Ratio of the number of peaches to the total number of fruits

= 3:10

The ratios above where a part is compared to the whole lot are called Part to Whole ratios.
