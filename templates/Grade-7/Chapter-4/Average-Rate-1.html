4.2.1 Average Rate
In a cricket match, player A scored 28 runs in 2 overs while player B scored 50 runs in 5 overs. Which player showed a better performance?
To find out their performances, calculate their scores per over. The number of runs scored in an over is termed as 'Run Rate'.
Run rate of player A
= 28 runs ÷ 2 overs

= 14 runs per over

Run rate of player B
= 50 runs ÷ 5 overs

= 10 runs per over
Player A scored 4 runs more than player B per over. Hence, player A performed better.
From the above example, we can define a rate as a ratio between two measurements with different units.
