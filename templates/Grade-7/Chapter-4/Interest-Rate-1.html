4.2.2 Interest Rate
When you borrow money from a bank, you pay the bank a fee to compensate for the use of borrowed money. Similarly, when you deposit money in a bank, the bank pays you a fee in return for the money you deposited. This fee is called interest.
Interest rate =

Interest
_____________________________

Amount of money deposited or borrowed

× 100
It is usually computed per annum i.e. per year. It is expressed as a percentage.
Example 18:
A man deposits $5500 in a bank for 2 years. If the interest rate is 4% per annum, how much total interest will he earn after 2 years if he withdraws interest at the end of each year?

Solution 18:
Interest for 2 years = $5500 × 4% × 2 = $440
Total amount of interest earned = $440
