Example 10:
If a : b = 3 : 4 and b : c = 5 : 6, find a : b : c.

Solution 10:
Note that b is common to the two given ratios. However, the values of b are different in the two ratios. If we combine the two ratios a : b and b : c into a single ratio a : b : c, we can have only one value for b. To achieve this, we convert each of the two given ratios to its equivalent ratio such that the value of b is the same in the two ratios. This is obtained by finding the LCM of the 2 original values of b.
